# Outreach Email Patterns

Five proven patterns for personalized outreach. Choose based on the lead's situation.

---

## Pattern 1: Inbound Warm

**When to use:** Lead filled a form, sent an inquiry, or directly contacted you.

**Subject:** Re: your {{form_name}} submission
**or:** Thanks for reaching out, {{first_name}}

**Body:**
```
Hey {{first_name}},

Saw your note about {{what_they_mentioned}}. Sounds like {{company}} is dealing with {{pain_point_they_described}}.

We've helped teams like {{similar_company}} solve exactly that — {{brief_value_prop}}.

Would it be worth a quick 15-min chat this week to see if we can help?

{{your_name}}
```

**Why it works:** Acknowledges their action, mirrors their language, low-pressure CTA.

---

## Pattern 2: Research-Based

**When to use:** You found something interesting about them or their company in your research.

**Subject:** Quick thought on {{their_initiative/product/announcement}}

**Body:**
```
Hi {{first_name}},

I came across {{specific_thing_you_found}} — really interesting approach to {{topic}}.

It got me thinking — teams working on {{their_focus}} often run into {{relevant_problem}}. That's exactly what we built {{your_product}} to solve.

Worth exploring? Happy to share how {{similar_company}} approached it.

{{your_name}}
```

**Why it works:** Shows genuine research, leads with insight not pitch, offers value.

---

## Pattern 3: Pain-Point

**When to use:** Clear problem match based on their role, industry, or form submission.

**Subject:** {{pain_point}} at {{company}}?

**Body:**
```
Hey {{first_name}},

Most {{their_role}}s at {{industry}} companies tell us {{common_pain_point}} is one of their biggest headaches.

We built {{your_product}} to fix that — {{one_sentence_how}}.

{{social_proof_sentence}} — would it make sense to show you how?

{{your_name}}
```

**Why it works:** Leads with their problem, not your product. Shows you understand their world.

---

## Pattern 4: Content-Based

**When to use:** The prospect published a blog post, gave a talk, appeared on a podcast, or shared content.

**Subject:** Loved your take on {{topic}}

**Body:**
```
Hi {{first_name}},

Just read your {{post/talk/podcast}} about {{topic}}. Your point about {{specific_insight}} really resonated.

We've been thinking about the same problem from the {{your_angle}} side. {{Brief connection to your product}}.

Would love to swap notes — any interest in a quick call?

{{your_name}}
```

**Why it works:** Genuine flattery that shows you actually consumed their content. Builds rapport before pitching.

---

## Pattern 5: Event-Based

**When to use:** Company recently had a notable event — funding round, acquisition, product launch, key hire, expansion.

**Subject:** Congrats on the {{event}}, {{first_name}}

**Body:**
```
Hey {{first_name}},

Saw the news about {{company}}'s {{event}} — congrats! That's a big milestone.

Teams in that growth phase usually start running into {{relevant_scaling_problem}}. We help companies like {{similar_company}} tackle that with {{brief_product_description}}.

Would it be useful to chat about how to avoid some common pitfalls as you scale?

{{your_name}}
```

**Why it works:** Timely, relevant, shows you follow their space. Positions you as helpful, not salesy.

---

## Universal Rules

1. **Never start with "I hope this email finds you well"** — instant delete trigger
2. **First sentence must be about THEM, not you** — hook with something specific
3. **One CTA only** — don't give them 3 things to do
4. **Keep it under 100 words** — respect their time
5. **No attachments on first touch** — builds trust first
6. **Subject line under 50 characters** — more opens on mobile
7. **Match their formality** — if they're casual, be casual. If corporate, be professional.
